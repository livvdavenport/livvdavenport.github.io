In college volleyball, there are usually a few stand out athletes. Most of the time these players are hitters that seem like a setter’s go-to option. In 2020, 2022 and 2023, one of these stand out athletes was Madi Skinner. Skinner, who is now a three-time National Champion and a recent LOVB champion made herself known in the college volleyball world.

Skinner reached champion status three times at two different universities. The first, Kentucky, where she was a freshman. Skinner played alongside her sister Avery Skinner when Kentucky won their first National Championship. There, she lurked in the shadows behind star outside Alli Stumler and her Sister Avery, but she was still utilized and as a freshman in the SEC, that’s impressive.

In 2022, Skinner transferred to the University of Texas. There she was able to relish in the spotlight. Skinner was a go to hitter for the Longhorns in their back-to-back National Championship wins. She helped lead the team to the title in 2022 and 2023. Is it a coincidence that three of the four teams Skinner was on won it all? And remember, this was at two different universities.

library(tidyverse)

kentucky <- season1 |>

Skinner was scoring points for all three of these teams and she was doing it efficiently. Defensively, Skinner can hold her own, but she truly shines on offense, which is to be expected of an outside hitter. But, there is something special about Skinner’s offense and her ability to mix up shots.

At Kentucky, Skinner’s usage was lower. Again, she was a freshman playing in the SEC, the second hardest conference in collegiate volleyball. The first: The Big Ten.

Texas is really when things started to amp up for Skinner. Both National Championship teams at Texas were very reliant on the right arm of Madi Skinner. In 2022, the only athlete to take more swings than her was Logan Eggleston. She was named the tournament’s most outstanding player. She was also the Big 12 player of the year three years in a row.

In Skinner’s second season at Texas, she was the star. Her total usage towered above the rest of her teammates and so did her kills. This was the year of Madi Skinner and she ended on top.

Alright, we get it. Skinner was a large part of the success of all three National Championship teams, but how good is she really compared to the rest of the outsides?

She’s still pretty damn good. Her freshman year, she was right in the middle of the road. She didn’t do anything extraordinary, but she was starting to carve a place for her name in NCAA volleyball.

In her first year at Texas, things started to pick up. She was being set and she was getting kills.

In her second year at Texas, when they won their second title in a row, Skinner was at the top of the food chain. Very few outsides were outsides were competing at the level she was. Unfortunately for Lexi Rodriguez and the Nebraska Cornhuskers, Skinner was nearly unstoppable, from the front row as well as the service line.

Skinner has since moved on from college volleyball. She is now on LOVB Austin. A team in the second professional league in the United States. LOVB consists of eight teams around the country and in their inaugural season, guess who won the title. Madi Skinner and LOVB Austin.

Maybe success follows Skinner or maybe she expects it. Four championships and counting for the young star.
